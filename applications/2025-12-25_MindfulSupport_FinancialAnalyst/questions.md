### How do you approach variance analysis?

> I follow a structured approach:
>
> 1. **Calculate the variance**: Actual vs. budget, actual vs. prior period, actual vs. forecast
> 2. **Identify the drivers**: Break it down‚Äîis it volume, price, timing, one-time items?
> 3. **Quantify the impact**: Not just "revenue was up" but "revenue increased $50K due to X"
> 4. **Tell the story**: What does leadership need to know? What's actionable?
>
> For example, at Thind Transport, I'd analyze monthly P&L variances and present them to leadership. If fuel costs spiked, I'd show how much, why (market prices vs. consumption), and what we could do about it. The goal isn't just explaining the numbers‚Äîit's providing insights that drive decisions.
