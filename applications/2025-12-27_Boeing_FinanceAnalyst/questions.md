### Tell me about your experience with variance analysis

> Variance analysis is one of my core skills:
>
> My approach:
> 1. **Calculate variances**: Actual vs. budget, actual vs. prior period
> 2. **Break down by driver**: Volume, rate, timing, one-time items
> 3. **Quantify impact**: Not just "costs were up" but "$X due to Y"
> 4. **Investigate root causes**: Why did this variance occur?
> 5. **Recommend actions**: What can we do about it?
>
> For example, at Thind Transport, when fuel costs spiked unexpectedly, I decomposed the variance into price (market rates) and volume (miles driven). I found that route inefficiency was driving excess mileage. That insight led to operational changes that reduced costs by 10%.
