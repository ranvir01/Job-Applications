### How do you break down complex accounting processes into automatable steps?

> I follow a structured approach:
>
> 1. **Map the current process**: What are the inputs? What transformations happen? What's the output? Who does what?
>
> 2. **Identify the pain points**: What's manual? What's error-prone? What takes the most time?
>
> 3. **Break into discrete steps**: Each step should have a clear trigger, action, and output. If a step is too complex, decompose further.
>
> 4. **Classify by automation potential**: Some steps are rule-based (high automation potential). Some require judgment (lower potential or need AI).
>
> 5. **Design the automation**: Map each step to the appropriate tool‚Äîformula, workflow rule, API call, AI agent.
>
> 6. **Test edge cases**: What happens when data is missing? When there are exceptions?
>
> **Example 1 - P&L Reporting**: I broke the process into: (1) data extraction from source systems, (2) categorization and mapping, (3) variance calculation, (4) exception flagging, (5) report generation. Each step became a module I could automate individually, then chain together.
>
> **Example 2 - Business Valuation**: For the Tabletop Village valuation, I decomposed a complex analysis into structured steps:
> - **Step 1: Financial Statement Analysis** (P&L review, Balance Sheet review, 4 years of data)
> - **Step 2: Financial Ratio Calculation** (liquidity, leverage, profitability ratios)
> - **Step 3: Industry Benchmarking** (compare ratios to industry averages)
> - **Step 4: EBITDA Adjustments** (normalize for owner salary, rent, interest, employee compensation)
> - **Step 5: Multiple Application** (apply industry EBITDA multiples)
> - **Step 6: Valuation Calculation** (range determination based on financial health)
>
> Each step had clear inputs, methodology, and outputs. The result was a $1.45M-$1.75M valuation with supporting analysis. That's the same discipline I'd use when configuring AI Agents‚Äîbreaking complex accounting workflows into structured, automatable steps.
