### OWNERSHIP: Tell me about a time you took ownership of a project beyond your defined responsibilities

**Task:** I wasn't explicitly asked to fix it, but I saw the inefficiency and decided to take ownership of the problem.

**Action:** I designed and built an automated reporting system using Excel VBA. I created macros for data extraction, built dynamic dashboards, standardized report formats, and documented the process for others.

**Result:** Reduced reporting time by **80%**. What took days became hours. The system is still in use, and I trained others to maintain it. I didn't wait to be asked‚ÄîI saw a problem and owned the solution.

### BIAS FOR ACTION: Tell me about a time you made a decision without having all the information

**Situation:** In my consulting work, a client needed a valuation quickly for a potential acquisition discussion. There was limited time to gather all historical financials.

**Task:** Deliver a credible valuation range within 3 days despite incomplete data.

**Action:** I used available data to build a valuation model with EBITDA multiples. Where data was missing, I made documented assumptions based on industry benchmarks. I built sensitivity analysis to show the range of outcomes under different scenarios.

**Result:** Delivered a $1.45M‚Äì$1.75M valuation range on time. The client used it in their discussions. I was transparent about assumptions, which built trust. Acting quickly with 80% of information was better than waiting for 100%.

---

### DIVE DEEP: Tell me about a time you had to dig into data to find the root cause of a problem

**Situation:** At Thind Transport, fuel costs spiked unexpectedly one quarter. Leadership wanted to know why.

**Task:** Identify the root cause and provide actionable recommendations.

**Action:** I analyzed the data at multiple levels: total fuel spend, cost per mile, gallons consumed, routes driven. I found that while fuel prices had increased modestly, the real driver was route inefficiency‚Äîlonger miles driven per delivery due to scheduling issues.

**Result:** Identified 15% excess mileage due to suboptimal routing. Recommended and implemented scheduling changes that reduced downtime by 15%. The dive-deep analysis turned a cost problem into an operational improvement.
