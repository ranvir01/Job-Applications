### How do you approach variance analysis?

> I follow a structured approach:
>
> 1. **Calculate the variance**: Actual vs. budget, actual vs. prior period
> 2. **Break it down**: Is it volume? Price/rate? Timing? One-time items?
> 3. **Quantify**: Not just "revenue was up" but "$50K favorable due to X"
> 4. **Prioritize**: Focus on material variances‚Äîwhat moves the needle?
> 5. **Communicate clearly**: Lead with the headline, support with detail
>
> For example, at Thind Transport, if fuel costs spiked, I'd show: (1) the dollar variance, (2) how much was market price vs. consumption, (3) what we could do about it. The goal is actionable insight, not just explanation.

### How do you communicate complex financial results to non-finance stakeholders?

> I think about my audience first. Executives and operators don't need the same detail as the finance team.

> My approach:
