### How do you analyze business performance and identify key drivers?

> I follow a structured approach:
>
> 1. **Define the metric**: What are we trying to understand? Revenue? Margin? Growth?
>
> 2. **Decompose into drivers**: Break the metric into its components. For revenue: volume √ó price. For costs: fixed + variable.
>
> 3. **Analyze trends**: How have these drivers changed over time? What's growing, declining, stable?
>
> 4. **Compare to benchmarks**: vs. budget, vs. prior period, vs. industry
>
> 5. **Identify root causes**: Why did this driver change? External factors? Internal decisions?
>
> 6. **Quantify impact**: How much of the total change does each driver explain?
>
> For example, at Thind Transport, when costs spiked, I decomposed into fuel, labor, maintenance. Found the real driver was route inefficiency (more miles per delivery), not just fuel prices. That insight led to operational changes that reduced costs.
